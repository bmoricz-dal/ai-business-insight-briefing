# Business Insight Briefing Prompt

Use the latest business source pack.

Select only the strongest 3 to 6 source items.

Write a practical AI business and market intelligence brief.

Tone:
- Clear
- Practical
- Skeptical
- Engaging
- Professional but easy to understand

Avoid jargon unless it is explained.

For each important signal, separate:

## Fact
What the source says.

## Meaning
What this likely means in business terms.

## Risk
What may be exaggerated, uncertain, incomplete, or vendor-biased.

## Application
How this could matter for AI adoption, business intelligence, workflow automation, FMCG, sales, distribution, or SMEs.

When using a technical term, explain it like this:

**Term:**  
**Plain English meaning:**  
**Business example:**  
**Why it matters:**  

Final brief structure:

# Practical AI Business & Market Intelligence Brief - YYYY-MM-DD

## 1. The short version

Five plain-English bullets. Each bullet should explain why it matters.

## 2. Best business signal

What happened? Why does it matter? What should a business learn?

## 3. AI implementation case

Who used AI? What problem were they trying to solve? What workflow or data was involved? What changed? What could go wrong?

## 4. Market intelligence note

What does this suggest about buyers, vendors, competitors, or adoption trends?

## 5. FMCG / sales / distribution angle

How could this matter for businesses involved in sales, distribution, retail, wholesale, or FMCG operations?

## 6. Method or framework of the day

Explain one useful concept simply.

Examples:
- Data readiness
- Human-in-the-loop
- Use-case scoring
- Workflow automation
- ROI logic
- Change management

## 7. Practical takeaway

What should a business reader take from this?

## 8. Follow-up research opportunity

What small research question could become a future brief?
